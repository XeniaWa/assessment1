The History of Peebles

In 1152, Peebles was made a Royal Burgh by King David the 1st.

One of the oldest buildings in Peebles, the Cross Kirk dates back to the 13th century and has existed almost as long as the town has been recognised a Royal Burgh. Originally a trinian priory which was built by order of King Alexander III due to the discovery of relics of Saint Nicholas of Myrna.

Another historical place of interest is Neidpath Castle. Dated to the late 1300s, Neidpath Castle is the resident royal building of the town and has been visited by such people as Sir Walter Scott and even Mary Queen of Scots. Regarded as a historical artifact of Peebles, the Castle slowly crumbled throughout the years but was restored to its former glory after the 1650s. Used against Cromwell in the mid-17th century, it is known as the strongest castle against Cromwell along the Forth River. Some interesting facts include that the roof was originally flat, and that the current castle was built on the ground of an older ruin, dated to the late 12th century.
